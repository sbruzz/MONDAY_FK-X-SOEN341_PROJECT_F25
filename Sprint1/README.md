#### 1. Meeting Minutes
**Location:** `Sprint1/MeetingMinutes/`

**Instructions:**
- Copy and rename the template files with actual meeting dates
- Fill in all sections for each meeting held
- Use the naming convention: `MONDAY_FK_Sprint1_##_YYYY-MM-DD.md`
- Example: `MONDAY_FK_Sprint1_01_2025-09-15.md`

**Required Information:**
- Date, time, location
- Attendees and absent members
- Discussion points
- Action items with assignees
- Next meeting details

#### 2. Individual Contribution Logs
**Location:** `Sprint1/ContributionLogs/`

**Instructions:**
- Each team member must fill out their own log
- Document ALL activities and time spent
- Update weekly throughout the sprint
- Be specific about what you worked on

**Required Information:**
- Activities performed with descriptions
- Time spent on each activity (in hours)
- Related GitHub issues/tasks (Task.##)
- Summary of key contributions
- Challenges faced and learnings

**Important Notes:**
- These logs are used to evaluate individual contributions
- Be honest and detailed about your work
- Include meeting time, research time, coding time, etc.
- Link activities to specific GitHub issues when possible

## Tips for Success

### For Meeting Minutes
- Assign one person to take notes during meetings
- Rotate the note-taker role among team members
- Document decisions made and rationale
- Track action items and follow up on them

### For Contribution Logs
- Update your log immediately after working on something
- Include small tasks too (they add up!)
- Mention collaboration with other team members
- Document learning activities (tutorials, research, etc.)

### Time Tracking Examples
- Attending team meeting: 1-2 hours
- Setting up development environment: 2-3 hours
- Implementing a feature: 4-8 hours
- Code review: 0.5-1 hour
- Bug fixing: 1-3 hours
- Documentation: 1-2 hours
- Research/learning: 1-4 hours

## Grading Criteria

Sprint 1 deliverables are evaluated based on:
- **Completeness**: All required files and information present
- **Quality**: Clear documentation, meaningful contributions
- **Traceability**: Activities linked to GitHub issues
- **Team Collaboration**: Evidence of regular meetings and coordination
- **Individual Contribution**: Each member's documented work
